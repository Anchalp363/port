Site Audit — Key Observations

What's working — and
what's costing you revenue

→
Hero slides without a clear CTA hierarchy The homepage runs three hero banners (Linen Co-ords, Rang Barse Sale, 5th Anniversary) in rotation. Each banner links to a collection — but there is no primary CTA button with contrast or text overlay that communicates a value proposition. Visitors land and scroll without a directed action.
→
Trust signals exist — but in the wrong positions Free Shipping, Free COD, and Easy Returns appear in the marquee ticker and inside the cart drawer. They do not appear on product pages below the ATC button — which is where purchase anxiety peaks. Repositioning these costs zero development time and can meaningfully reduce drop-off.
→
198 kaftans with no filtering by fabric, length, or occasion The Kaftan collection alone has 198 products — a number that creates choice paralysis without robust filters. There is no way to filter by Short vs Long, Satin vs Cotton, Print style, or Occasion. High-intent shoppers who can't find what they want within two scrolls abandon to competitors.
→
No AOV optimisation at cart stage The cart drawer shows items, COD badge, shipping info, and a checkout button. It does not attempt to increase order value through upsell recommendations, free gift progress bars, or "complete the look" suggestions. Given the brand's breadth of catalogue, this is the highest-ROI untapped lever.
→
1,631 reviews are not visible during the buying decision Customer reviews are displayed on the homepage "Style with Aarna" section, and on a dedicated Reviews page. But on product pages — where purchase decisions are made — review integration needs to be more prominent, visible above the fold, and include photos to drive the final conversion.
→
NGO story and brand equity are underutilised The blog post "Your purchase contributes to an NGO for handicapped people" has real emotional power. Yet it receives no surface area on the homepage hero, no mention on product pages, and no callout at checkout. For a segment of shoppers, this story can be the deciding factor — but they never see it.

Exit Intent Recovery
Visitors who have added items to cart but are about to leave represent the highest-intent abandonment segment.
